1. Shopping Case

Aman has ₹500. He buys a bag for ₹225, a book for ₹120, and a lunch box for ₹85.

a) Total money spent = ₹225 + ₹120 + ₹85 = ₹430

b) Money left = ₹500 − ₹430 = ₹70

c) If he wants to buy a water bottle costing ₹90, does he have enough money? No, because he has only ₹70 left.

2. Time and Speed Case

Ravi cycles at a speed of 10 km per hour. He travels for 2.5 hours.

a) Distance travelled = 10 × 2.5 = 25 km

b) If he rests for 30 minutes, how much total time did he spend? 3 hours

c) If he increases his speed to 12 km per hour for 1 hour, how much distance will he cover? 12 km

3. Measurement Case

The wire is 12.5 meters long. 3.75 meters is cut from it.

a) Length remaining = 12.5 − 3.75 = 8.75 m

b) If another 2.5 meters is cut, how much is left? 8.75 − 2.5 = 6.25 m

c) Convert the remaining length into centimeters. 6.25 × 100 = 625 cm

4. Ratio Case

In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 3 : 2. There are 30 students in total.

a) How many boys are there? 18

b) How many girls are there? 12

c) If 5 more girls join, what will be the new ratio? 18 : 17

5. Fractions and Decimals Case

A bottle contains 2.5 liters of juice. Riya drinks 0.75 liters and her brother drinks 0.5 liters.

a) Total juice consumed = 0.75 + 0.5 = 1.25 L

b) Juice remaining = 2.5 − 1.25 = 1.25 L

c) Express the remaining juice as a fraction. 5/4 L (1¼ L)

6. Data Handling Case

The marks obtained by 5 students are: 45, 60, 55, 70, 50.

a) Highest marks = 70

b) Lowest marks = 45

c) Average marks = (45 + 60 + 55 + 70 + 50) ÷ 5 = 56

7. Geometry Case

A rectangular garden has length 15 m and breadth 10 m.

a) Perimeter = 2 × (15 + 10) = 50 m

b) Area = 15 × 10 = 150 m²

c) If grass costs ₹4 per square meter, what is the total cost to cover the garden? 150 × 4 = ₹600
